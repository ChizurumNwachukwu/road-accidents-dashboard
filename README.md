## 📌 Project Overview
As part of a data analytics project, I was tasked as a Data Scientist to perform 
statistical analysis and build an interactive Excel dashboard to help stakeholders 
derive actionable insights from UK road accident data spanning 2021 and 2022.

## ❗ Problem Statement
Road accidents remain a critical public safety concern. Raw accident data often 
exists in unstructured formats, making it difficult for stakeholders to identify 
patterns. This project addresses that by cleaning, analysing, and visualising the data.

## 🎯 Objectives
- Clean and standardise the accident dataset (handle missing, inconsistent, duplicate values)
- Analyse frequency and severity of accidents (Fatal, Serious, Slight)
- Identify high-risk factors: speed limits, road conditions, weather, time of day
- Perform descriptive statistical analysis
- Build an interactive Excel dashboard with filters and dynamic visualisations
- Provide data-driven recommendations for road safety improvements

## 🔍 Key Findings
- **Daylight accidents are more serious** than night-time accidents,  likely due to higher traffic volumes and driver overconfidence
- **Rural areas** see more severe accidents due to higher speeds, poorer road conditions, and longer emergency response times
- **30mph zones** record the most accidents, driven by urban traffic density and mixed road users
- **Dry roads** account for the majority of casualties (279.4k) driver overconfidence plays a key role
- **Fine weather** also sees high accident rates, again linked to overconfidence and distraction
